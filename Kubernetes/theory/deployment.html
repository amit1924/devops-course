üîô Rollback Explained

Suppose v2 has a bug:

kubectl rollout undo deployment hello-deploy

Kubernetes does:

Scales up old ReplicaSet (v1)

From 0 to 10 replicas

Scales down new ReplicaSet (v2)

From 10 to 0 replicas

üîÑ

Reverse of rollout

Zero downtime again

Why Old ReplicaSet Stays?

Because rollback needs it!

Kubernetes keeps old ReplicaSets (config stored) even with 0 Pods

üéØ Simple Rule To Remember

Scaling

"How many?"

  • Quantity change
  • Same ReplicaSet
  • No version change

Rolling Update

"Which version?"

  • Version/config change
  • New ReplicaSet
  • Gradual replacement

Clean separation = powerful architecture
